NLP & Clustering on Magic: The Gathering

Overview

This project applies Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Unsupervised Learning techniques to the complex dataset of "Magic: The Gathering" cards. The goal is to move beyond simple color-based classification and understand the deep semantic relationships between deck archetypes.

> ./methodology --analyze

1. Data Extraction: Scraped 20,000+ unique card texts and tournament decklists.

2. Vectorization: Utilized TF-IDF and Word2Vec to convert card rules text into numerical vectors, capturing mechanics like "Counter target spell" or "Destroy creature".

3. Dimensionality Reduction: Applied PCA and t-SNE to visualize the high-dimensional card space, revealing distinct clusters of "Control", "Aggro", and "Combo" strategies that align with game theory archetypes.

> ./results

The analysis revealed that in certain "Eternal" formats, the distinction between "Midrange" and "Control" decks is topologically non-existent, suggesting a continuous spectrum of control rather than discrete categories.
